The Geoscientist
Behind the Research

I am Zitu Kundu, a graduate researcher at the Department of Geology & Mining, University of Barishal. My MS thesis investigates the geochemical fingerprint of Kuakata Beach — Bangladesh's iconic bayshore coastline facing the Bay of Bengal.

My work employs major oxide and trace element geochemistry, combined with statistical source apportionment (EPA PMF 5.0), to decode the provenance, tectonic setting, and weathering history encoded in coastal sand. By integrating field sampling with ArcGIS spatial analysis and Python-based data pipelines, I translate raw geochemical data into meaningful geological narratives.

With two peer-reviewed papers published in Elsevier journals and a third manuscript under review at a Q1 journal, I am building an international research profile while actively preparing for graduate studies abroad.
