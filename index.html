01 — Profile

About Me

I am Zitu Kundu, a graduate researcher at the Department of Geology & Mining, University of Barishal, Bangladesh. My MS thesis — currently defended — is titled "A Comprehensive Geochemical Investigation of the Kuakata Beach Sand: Implications for Provenance, Weathering, Heavy Metal Contamination, and Natural Radioactivity."

My research works with major oxides, trace elements, and rare earth elements (REEs) to constrain sediment provenance, weathering history, and geochemical conditions of the Bengal coastal system. I also conduct radiological risk assessment, health risk assessment, and spatial distribution analysis of heavy metals and NORMs.

Beyond my thesis, I am actively developing manuscripts on the Brahmaputra, Punarbhaba, and Dhaleswari River systems, and working on a cyclone vulnerability assessment for Cox's Bazar. My broader research interests include geochemistry, sedimentology, coastal hydrology, environmental pollution, climate change, and GIS & remote sensing.

I am currently preparing for a second IELTS attempt and building my profile for higher studies and research positions abroad.
